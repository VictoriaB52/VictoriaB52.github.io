In today's day and age, video games are one of the most popular forms of media and entertainment, accounting for $57.2 billion dollars of US Spending in 2023 alone (Source).

But how did we get to this point, and what contributed to the growth of the market? These are the questions this narrative visualization will explore. We will use the Video Games Sales Dataset, available publically at Kaggle.

We begin with a timelapse of video game sales in 4 regions (North America, Europe, Japan, and Other), as well as worldwide sales. Afterwards, feel free to use the header nav to explore the dataset

Above are plotted the sales for each region over time. In this format, we can see the progression and at times, regression of sales. The annotations provide some potential explanations for these developments

If you're interested in seeing how overall trends for video game sales differ based on various game attributes, use the filters on the left-hand side to explore these differences

Additionally, click on a given plot point on the graph to drill into the performance of games in that particular year and region
